Business Overview

Two complementary ventures — Allure is the core AI marketplace; Zupra is the distribution-and-cash-flow engine that reduces Allure's CAC while generating independent revenue from Day 1.

Allure Interiors — AI Marketplace
  • Connects homeowners ↔ verified interior designers via AI preference matching
  • Invite-only curated network — 30–40% of designer applicants rejected
  • Style quiz → AI ranks Top 3–5 matched designers per client
  • Builder partnership channel for warm lead acquisition
  • Institutional consortium model for IIT/NIT/IIM projects (Phase 2)
  • Revenue: 5–8% commission on residential; 2–3% institutional
  • Model: Asset-light marketplace — NOT managed service
Zupra — Adtech (Physical Medium)
  • Premium branded water bottles as B2B advertising medium
  • Distributed in corporate offices, builder offices, co-working spaces
  • Advertisers pay per placement cycle (bottle batch + distribution)
  • Allure is Zupra's first advertiser — its own CAC reduction mechanism
  • Standalone ad revenue from interior brands, fintech, lifestyle
  • Revenue: ₹8K–₹25K per advertiser per placement cycle
  • Cash flow: upfront payment before bottle production

Why This Is a High-Margin Business

Marketplace = SaaS-like margins
  • No delivery overhead, no staff designers, no inventory
  • 87% gross margin per project at base case
  • Livspace gross margin: 35–45% (managed service overhead)
  • Every additional project above break-even is ~87 paisa of profit per rupee
Dual revenue = capital efficiency
  • Zupra generates cash from Month 1 before Allure has a single client
  • ₹20L pre-seed buys 8+ months of runway (vs. 3–4 months single-venture)
  • Effective burn rate: ₹1.2–1.8L/month (not ₹3–5L for typical startup)
  • Two revenue streams from one BD effort = best-in-class capital efficiency
Data flywheel = long-term moat
  • Every match generates preference + outcome data that improves AI accuracy
  • Year 3: 5,000+ matched projects = India's only interior preference dataset
  • This dataset is a standalone asset (licensing to furniture brands, paint companies)
  • First-mover with real match data cannot be replicated by latecomer

Why Investors Should Bet on This Team

The model works — we just need to execute. myGubbi and HipCouch proved the curated design marketplace demand exists in India. They failed to break out because they lacked AI matching, a builder distribution channel, and a 25-person parallel execution team. Allure has all three from Day 1.
