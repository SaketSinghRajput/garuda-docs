01

Company Overview

Zupra is building an Offline Engagement Infrastructure Layer for Physical Commerce. The company transforms existing consumer packaging and distribution networks into measurable, attributable engagement channels for brands, institutions, and businesses.

Market entry begins through bottled water and packaging-based campaigns. The long-term vision is to establish a measurable offline media and interaction network spanning FMCG products, food packaging, retail distribution, educational institutions, corporate ecosystems, and consumer touchpoints across India.

The Problem

Digital advertising costs are rising steeply. Offline brand interactions remain largely unmeasured, unattributed, and disconnected from digital analytics. Physical consumer touchpoints — particularly packaging — are wasted impressions.

The Opportunity

Billions of FMCG units pass through consumer hands each year. QR adoption has been normalised through UPI across Tier-1, 2, and 3 India. Zupra converts this physical surface area into a trackable, data-rich engagement medium.

04

Dynamic Visibility Bottle Initiative

The Dynamic Visibility Bottle is a consumption-triggered interactive packaging system. The QR code is hidden or visually distorted while the bottle is full. As the water level decreases, optical distortion reduces and the QR code gradually becomes visible and scannable.

This mechanism creates a natural, curiosity-driven engagement loop tied to the act of consumption — increasing attention, recall, and scan intent without relying on external prompts.

Potential BenefitMechanismValidation Status
Curiosity-Driven Engagement QR reveal as water is consumed creates novelty and anticipation Hypothesis
Higher Attention Retention Gradual reveal sustains consumer attention across the consumption period Hypothesis
Improved Brand Recall Interactive packaging creates stronger memory imprint vs. static media Hypothesis
Superior Advertiser ROI Higher effective scan rates vs. static QR on conventional packaging Hypothesis
Premium Campaign Positioning Novel format enables differentiated pricing and exclusive advertiser positioning Hypothesis

Important: The Dynamic Visibility Bottle concept remains in pilot-stage validation. No claims of commercial viability are made at this stage. All stated benefits are hypotheses to be tested during the funded pilot. Conventional static QR packaging serves as a validated fallback.

Technology Development

Zupra's technology layer is the primary source of long-term defensibility. While the initial product appears as a packaging medium, the underlying platform is a campaign management, attribution, and analytics infrastructure that becomes more valuable as distribution scale increases.

ComponentDescriptionStrategic Value
QR Infrastructure Dynamic QR codes linked to campaign-specific landing pages; unique identifiers per distribution point and bottle batch Granular attribution and fraud prevention
Campaign Management Advertiser-facing tool to create, schedule, and manage campaigns across distribution points and geographies Reduces CAC; enables self-serve at scale
Advertiser Dashboard Real-time reporting on impressions, scans, engagement rate, and audience demographics by location and segment Core retention driver; justifies repeat spend
Analytics Engine Processes engagement data to produce CPM, CPC, scan-through rate, and audience behaviour insights Enables performance-based pricing models
Attribution Reporting Links physical scan events to downstream digital actions (website visits, app downloads, form fills) Bridges offline-to-online gap; key differentiator
Engagement Tracking Time-stamped scan logs with geolocation, device type, and repeat visitor identification Proprietary data asset; improves targeting over time

Closing Statement

Zupra is not building a bottle advertising business. The long-term vision is to create a measurable offline engagement infrastructure that connects physical consumer interactions with digital attribution, analytics, and customer acquisition — at scale across India's vast physical commerce network.

The current round is designed to validate the core assumptions required to build that infrastructure responsibly. Capital will be deployed against specific, measurable validation objectives with clear success criteria.

This is a validation-stage investment. The objective is not to demonstrate scale, but to demonstrate that the model deserves to be scaled. The founders are committed to disciplined capital allocation, transparent reporting, and honest communication of outcomes — positive or negative.
