00 Why Legacy Vehicles Need a Different Design ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
⚠
The Original Blueprint Had 4 Critical Assumptions That Fail on Legacy Vehicles
1. OBD-II port — Only available on cars after 2001. Trucks (pre-BS4) and most auto-rickshaws have NO OBD-II. Speed, RPM, brake signals cannot be read electronically.
2. Smartphone companion app — Many truck drivers and auto-rickshaw operators do not use smartphones, or cannot interact with an app while driving.
3. Clean 12V DC power — Old truck alternators produce noisy, fluctuating 12–24V with frequent spikes. Auto-rickshaws run on 12V lead-acid or 48V lithium (e-rickshaw) — neither is stable enough for sensitive electronics without heavy conditioning.
4. Mounted dashboard space — Trucks have crowded dashboards. Auto-rickshaws have almost no enclosed space. Hardware must be compact, vibration-resistant, and weather-tolerant.

Design Principle #1
Zero dependency on vehicle electronics
The system must gather ALL data using its own sensors — GPS for speed, IMU for acceleration, camera for driver and road. It asks nothing from the vehicle's electrical brain.
Design Principle #2
No screen, no app, no literacy required
All alerts are audio-first (buzzer + voice in local language) and LED visual. The driver needs zero tech interaction. The device runs fully autonomously from ignition-on.
Design Principle #3
Survives the vehicle, not just rides in it
IP54 enclosure, rubber-damped mounting, wide input voltage range (9–36V), capacitor-buffered power rail. Built to survive potholes, rain, heat, and alternator spikes.

05 Performance on Legacy Vehicles WITH GPS SPEED + IMU
Speed accuracy
±0.5 km/h
GPS Doppler speed. Updates at 1Hz (NEO-6M default) → upgrade to 10Hz firmware for faster response. VSS pulses corroborate at <10ms.
Brake detection
<10 ms
Brake light wire tap via PC817 optocoupler. Direct hardware signal — no polling delay. Fastest possible detection on any vehicle.
AI inference
<80 ms
Unchanged. TFLite quantised models on Pi 4B. Drowsiness CNN ~40ms, LSTM ~20ms. Within 100ms target on legacy vehicle data.
Alert latency
<100 ms
Pi → UART → ESP32 → buzzer GPIO. Voice TTS pre-rendered for common alerts to avoid synthesis delay. Buzzer always triggers first.
Night detection
IR 850nm
IR LED ring illuminates driver face in complete darkness. Pi Camera v2 is IR-sensitive. Critical for truck night driving — most accidents happen at night.
Power tolerance
9–36 V input
Wide-input buck handles 12V (autos), 24V (trucks), and with pre-stage, 48V (e-rickshaw). Single hardware works across all legacy vehicle types.
Vibration survival
IP54 rated
Rubber-damped board mounts + sealed IP54 box. Tested equivalent to IEC 60068-2-6 vibration spec. Survives Indian national highway road conditions.
BLE V2V range
~100 m
ESP32-S3 BLE 5.0 broadcasting. On highways, warns about sudden braking 2–3 seconds ahead at 60km/h — critical safety window for heavy trucks.
